Paneer Bhurji

What is Paneer Bhurji?

Paneer Bhurji is a popular North Indian dish made from crumbled paneer (Indian cottage cheese) cooked with onions, tomatoes, spices, and herbs. “Bhurji” means scrambled, so this is essentially a scrambled paneer dish — spicy, savory, and satisfying. It's a go-to recipe for a quick lunch or dinner, often paired with chapati, paratha, or bread. It's also commonly served as a filling in wraps or sandwiches.

Ingredients

  • Paneer: Fresh, crumbled cottage cheese
  • Onions: Finely chopped
  • Tomatoes: Finely chopped
  • Green chilies: Chopped
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Cumin seeds
  • Turmeric powder
  • Red chili powder or deggi mirch
  • Garam masala
  • Salt
  • Oil or ghee
  • Cilantro: Freshly chopped for garnish

Cooking the Paneer Bhurji

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. Then add green chilies and ginger-garlic paste. Cook until fragrant.
  3. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft and pulpy. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  4. Cook the masala until oil starts separating from the sides.
  5. Add crumbled paneer to the masala. Mix everything well so paneer is coated evenly.
  6. Cook for 2–3 minutes. Avoid overcooking as paneer can turn rubbery.
  7. Sprinkle garam masala and chopped cilantro. Give it a final stir and turn off the heat.
  8. Serve hot with chapati, paratha, or bread. Can also be used in wraps or sandwiches.
